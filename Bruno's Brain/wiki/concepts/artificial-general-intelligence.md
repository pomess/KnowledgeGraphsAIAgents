# Artificial General Intelligence (AGI)

Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) refers to hypothetical AI systems that possess human-level cognitive abilities across a wide range of tasks, rather than being specialized in a narrow domain. This includes abilities such as reasoning, problem-solving, learning from experience, understanding complex ideas, and adapting to new situations.

## Distinction from Artificial Superintelligence (ASI)

AGI is often considered a precursor to [[artificial-superintelligence|Artificial Superintelligence (ASI)]]. While AGI matches human intellect, [[artificial-superintelligence|ASI]] surpasses it in virtually every cognitive dimension, including creativity, general wisdom, and problem-solving. The [[ai-2027]] scenario, for instance, outlines a path where rapid self-improvement through an [[intelligence-explosion]] quickly transitions from AGI capabilities to ASI within months.

## Role in Intelligence Explosion

The development of AGI is a critical milestone on the path to an [[intelligence-explosion]]. Once an AI system reaches general human-level intelligence, it could theoretically automate and accelerate its own research and development, leading to a rapid increase in capabilities. This self-improvement loop is a central mechanism described in the [[ai-2027]] scenario, where a "superhuman researcher" (a form of advanced AGI) is a key step before the emergence of [[artificial-superintelligence|ASI]].

## Alignment Challenges

The emergence of AGI significantly escalates the [[ai-alignment|AI alignment problem]]. Ensuring that an AGI's goals and values are aligned with human intent becomes paramount, as its general capabilities could lead to unforeseen and potentially catastrophic outcomes if misaligned.
