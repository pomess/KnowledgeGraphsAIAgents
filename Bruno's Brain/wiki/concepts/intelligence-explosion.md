# Intelligence Explosion

The hypothesis that once AI systems become capable enough to improve their own design, a feedback loop emerges: better AI → faster AI research → even better AI → even faster research, etc. The result is a rapid, potentially uncontrollable acceleration in AI capability.

The term originates from I.J. Good (1965): "an ultraintelligent machine could design even better machines; there would then unquestionably be an 'intelligence explosion,' and the intelligence of man would be left far behind."

## Mechanism (per AI 2027)

The [[ai-2027]] scenario provides the most detailed concrete model of how this might unfold:

1. **AI automates coding** — AI agents handle software engineering tasks, speeding up AI R&D by 1.5x (early 2026).
2. **AI automates research engineering** — AI designs and runs experiments autonomously, reaching 3x-4x multiplier (early 2027).
3. **AI automates research taste** — AI develops the judgment to decide *what* to research, not just *how* to implement it, reaching 10x (mid 2027).
4. **AI surpasses humans entirely** — AI is better than all humans at all AI research tasks. 50x multiplier, limited mainly by compute for running experiments (late 2027).

The key metric is the [[ai-r-and-d-progress-multiplier]] — how much faster AI makes algorithmic progress compared to humans alone.

## Timeline in AI 2027

| Date | Multiplier | Character |
|------|-----------|-----------|
| Early 2026 | 1.5x | Agent-1: good at coding, unreliable at long tasks |
| Jan 2027 | 3x | Agent-2: near-expert research engineer |
| Mar 2027 | 4x | Agent-3: [[superhuman-coder]], bottleneck shifts to research taste |
| Jun 2027 | 10x | Agent-3 improved: "country of geniuses in a datacenter" |
| Sep 2027 | 50x | Agent-4: superhuman at all AI research, bottlenecked on compute |

## Why It Matters

If the intelligence explosion occurs, the period between human-level AI and vastly superhuman AI could be measured in months rather than decades. This compresses the window for solving [[ai-alignment]] from "we have years" to "we have weeks." It also creates acute geopolitical pressure — the [[ai-arms-race]] means neither side can afford to slow down.

## Key Uncertainty

The authors note that their forecast beyond 2026 is "substantially less grounded" than the near-term predictions. The compounding dynamics are "inherently much less predictable." They think the timeline could be ~5x slower or faster than depicted.
