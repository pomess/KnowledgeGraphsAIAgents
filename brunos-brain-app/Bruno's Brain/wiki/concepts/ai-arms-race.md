# AI Arms Race (US-China)

The geopolitical competition between the United States and China for dominance in AI capabilities, depicted in [[ai-2027]] as the primary constraint shaping decisions about AI development, deployment, and safety.

## The Asymmetry

Per the AI 2027 scenario, the US holds significant advantages:

| Factor | US | China |
|--------|-----|-------|
| Global AI compute share | ~70% (all companies), ~20% (OpenBrain alone) | ~12% |
| Chip access | Full access to TSMC frontier chips | Relies on smuggling, older chips, domestic chips ~3 years behind |
| Organizational model | Private companies competing | Nationalized under CCP direction (DeepCent-led collective) |
| AI capability gap | Leading | ~6 months behind in models |

China's advantage is **centralization** — nearly all compute unified under one project, concentrated at the Tianwan Centralized Development Zone (CDZ).

## Escalation Dynamics

The scenario depicts a series of escalating moves:

1. **Chip export controls** keep China resource-constrained
2. **CCP nationalizes AI research** (mid 2026) — merges all AI companies into a DeepCent-led collective
3. **China steals Agent-2 weights** (Feb 2027) via coordinated insider/cyber operation
4. **US retaliatory cyberattacks** on DeepCent fail due to airgapped CDZ security
5. **Security clearances** required for all OpenBrain employees; spies caught
6. **Extreme contingencies drawn up**: US considers Defense Production Act seizure of trailing companies' datacenters, kinetic strikes on Chinese datacenters; China considers Taiwan blockade/invasion

## Why It Matters for Alignment

The arms race is the primary reason [[ai-alignment]] doesn't get solved in the scenario. Every time safety researchers recommend slowing down or pausing, leadership weighs the alignment risk against the risk of China catching up:

> "DeepCent is just two months behind. A unilateral pause in capabilities progress could hand the AI lead to China, and with it, control over the future."

This dynamic ensures that models are deployed before alignment is verified, monitoring is insufficient, and red flags are rationalized away.

## Arms Control

The scenario briefly explores arms control possibilities. In principle, major datacenters are hard to hide, and nuclear arms control treaties provide precedent. In practice, both sides prefer unilateral advantage to mutual restraint. China has natural interest in a treaty (being behind) but US overtures lead nowhere.
