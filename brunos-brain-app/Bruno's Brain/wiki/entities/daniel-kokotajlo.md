# Daniel Kokotajlo

Former OpenAI researcher, named to TIME100 and profiled by the New York Times. Lead author of [[ai-2027]].

## Background

Kokotajlo left OpenAI and became a prominent voice on AI risk. His previous AI forecasting work — "What 2026 Looks Like" (August 2021, LessWrong) — successfully predicted chain-of-thought reasoning, inference scaling, chip export controls, and $100M training runs, all more than a year before ChatGPT launched. This track record gives his forecasts more weight than typical speculation.

## Key Positions

- AI progress is moving faster than most people expect
- The [[intelligence-explosion]] is a near-term possibility, not a distant hypothetical
- [[ai-alignment|Alignment]] is an unsolved problem that will become critical as systems approach superhuman capability
- Modal estimate (at time of publication): [[artificial-general-intelligence|AGI]] arrives ~2027

## Role in AI 2027

Lead author and primary forecaster for the [[ai-2027]] scenario. His OpenAI experience informs the document's detailed depictions of internal AI lab dynamics, security practices, and the relationship between AI companies and government.
